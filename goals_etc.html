Why did I make this website?

If you're on this page, your probably interested in why I wanted to make this website in the first place, so I will try to explain it all here (if you're not interested in that, feel free to go to any of the other pages on this website! :)

Background: Why I needed something like a website

So now a very long time ago I was looking into hosting a website because I wanted a place to centralise all my little projects, because it was getting out of hand and I didn't have a clear overview anymore. I knew that my internet provider had some options, but of course around a year before I looked into it they stopped that service. Instead I just tried to tidy up everything as much as I could and that worked okay.
A few years later, when looking into better ways to take notes in class - I had been using a combination of a reusable notebook (by Rocketbook) and OneNote. The reason for both and not one of them is because I didn't like how OneNote's interface worked, and that was solvable with good ol' pen and paper, but the thing about that is that it's bulky and more importantly quite wastefull. So with that solution I could write my notes physically in class and then upload them to OneNote to do the actual studying at home from there.
That worked okay for a bit, but OneNote kinda sucks to be honest, so then my brother introduced me to Obsidian, the (imo) best note taking app created to date. For those of you that do not know about it, Obsidian's whole thing is that you work with links, and since we are humans with lives that inevitably have recurring things happening, links are super useful. But especially at uni this was amazing. Have a concept that keeps being mentioned? Great! Make a note about it, link to it in the notes of your class your taking, and next time you hear it, just put in a little [[conceptname]] and you're good to go, you get reminded what it means, you get a connection between that class, the concept and the other class where the concept was mentioned. Honestly I could talk for so long about this.
Obsidian was so good in fact that it could host many of my different idea's, projects, studies, etc. So my need for something like a website deminished for a bit. But of course it cannot do everything, that brings me onto the second part of why I wanted a website:

Background: Why I wanted something public and everlasting

This way of phrasing it probably kind of spoils the second reason, and that is my love for history. Wait, maybe that made it more confusing, so let me explain. So I have a master in History and a master in Archaeology, I like the very human things about history and about our past, but what keeps coming up when studying history, is the lack of primary sources. That's why I started studying archaeology in the first place (not only reason but that's for another time), because history just didn't offer enough, one always reaches dead ends. But all of my disapointment or complaining about people in the past destroying or just not leaving any evidence behind, is very hypocritical if I myself literally do the same thing constantly. When I started realising this, I immediately started writing more and taking more photo's. Of course I can always do more, but it has been a great improvement compared to before. The logical next step is to make everything that approximates the story of my life (that makes it sound so grandiose, but I just mean it in a historical sense) more available for who's looking for it.
Now I am aware of the whole privacy aspect of what I am talking about here, and I hope to eventually get around to writing about it in my little manifesto, but in short, I believe most massive (social media) corporations already know so much about us, maybe more than we know about ourselves, that doing it in a controlled, aware, and transparent way is a lot more healthy. I don't mean to replace social media entirely, but I mean to reframe it, right now these algorithms trained on feeding us advertisements know so much about our habits it's frankly scary, but saying something in a wholy personal and unique way is very different. This second thing is where my website comes in.
The goals summarized: I want a centralized, everywhere accessible way to store my projects, all the while making it possible for those interested to take part in my life.

ik wil mijn website als een reis maken doorheen een wereld - mijn wereld. Mijn idee op dit moment is dat de website start op een pagina waar een kasteel te zien is in de achtergrond. Ik wil alles klikbaar maken, maar ook een soort "moc" hebben, gezet in de virtuele wereld, waarop je meteen naar elke pagina kan gaan. Dit zal duidelijk worden wanneer ik alle pagina's overloop.
It's interesting how many of my favorite genres deal with sampling. Trip hop, Uk Garage, techno, ambient dubstep, jungle, etc. all use samples to varying degrees. Some use it as a small addition, some rely on it heavily to the extent that whole songs are completely comprised of samples, from the beat, to the looping vocals, to the melodies. What I am most interested in is how samples can be transformed to the point that they become unrecognisable; sure it is very satisfying to know a reference and understand where the sample came from just by listening to a song for the first time, but learning how the artist radically changed the context or sound of a sample hits the spot for me.
Why am I talking about music in the "goals" page of my website? Well, I want to treat my website in a similar way as some of these artists. I want my website to be filled with sprites, images, gifs, music, poems, videos, and soundbites from all corners of my life, the internet and everything. I want some to be very obvious references, and others to be so transformed that they're barely unrecognisable - and all of this combined with my original goal of making a website to host my many projects, reviews, blogs, etc.
